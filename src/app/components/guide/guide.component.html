Guides

Before

Prepare an Emergency Kit Gather essential supplies like food, water, flashlight, and first aid kit. Create an Emergency Plan Establish a communication plan with your family and designate a safe meeting place. Secure Heavy Furniture Anchor tall furniture and secure heavy objects to prevent them from toppling during an earthquake.

During

Drop, Cover, and Hold On If indoors, take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture and protect your head and neck. Stay Away from Windows Avoid standing near windows or glass that could shatter and cause injuries. Be Prepared for Aftershocks Stay alert for aftershocks, which are smaller earthquakes that may occur after the main quake.

After

Check for Injuries Assess yourself and others for any injuries and provide immediate first aid if needed. Inspect Your Surroundings Check for hazards like gas leaks, damaged electrical lines, or structural damage before entering buildings. Contact Loved Ones Reach out to family and friends to let them know you are safe and inquire about their well-being.

Before

Secure Outdoor Objects Bring in or secure outdoor furniture, plants, and other objects that can be blown away. Stock Up on Supplies Ensure you have enough food, water, batteries, and necessary supplies to last several days. Know Your Evacuation Route Familiarize yourself with the designated evacuation routes in your area.

During

Stay Indoors and Seek Shelter Remain indoors and take shelter in a small, windowless room on the lowest level of your home. Monitor Weather Updates Keep updated with the latest weather forecasts and emergency announcements. Stay Away from Windows and Doors Avoid standing near windows or glass that could break due to high winds.

After

Assess Damage Safely Only enter or approach damaged buildings or areas once it is declared safe by authorities. Check on Neighbors Reach out to neighbors, especially those who may require assistance or support. Listen to Official Instructions Follow instructions from local authorities regarding cleanup, repairs, and return to normalcy.

Before

Know the Flood Risk in Your Area Understand the likelihood of floods in your region and be aware of flood warning systems. Create a Family Emergency Plan Establish evacuation routes, a communication plan, and a designated meeting point for your family. Elevate Valuables and Important Documents Raise important items off the ground or store them in waterproof containers.

During

Follow Evacuation Orders If instructed to evacuate, leave immediately and follow designated evacuation routes. Avoid Walking or Driving Through Floodwater Stay away from flooded areas and do not attempt to cross submerged roads or bridges. Move to Higher Ground Seek higher elevation if necessary, and stay away from rivers, streams, and other bodies of water.

After

Wait for the All Clear Wait until authorities declare it safe to return home or enter affected areas. Document Damages and Contact Insurance Take photographs of the damage for insurance purposes and contact your insurance provider. Be Cautious of Contaminated Water and Hazards Avoid contact with floodwater as it may be contaminated, and be aware of potential hazards caused by the flood.

Before

Follow Traffic Rules and Regulations Adhere to speed limits, traffic signals, and maintain a safe distance from other vehicles. Maintain Your Vehicle Ensure your vehicle is in good condition with regular maintenance, including brakes and tires. Wear Seat Belts and Use Child Restraints Always wear seat belts while driving and use appropriate child safety seats.

During

Stay Calm and Assess the Situation Remain calm, check for injuries, and evaluate the safety of yourself and others involved. Move Vehicles to a Safe Location, if Possible If it is safe to do so, move vehicles out of the way of traffic to prevent further collisions or hazards. Contact Emergency Services Call emergency services to report the collision and request medical assistance if needed.

After

Exchange Information with Other Parties Exchange contact and insurance information with other involved parties. Report the Collision to Authorities and Your Insurance Provider File a police report and inform your insurance provider about the collision as soon as possible. Seek Medical Attention, if Necessary If you or anyone involved is injured, seek medical attention for proper evaluation and treatment.

Before

Ensure Building Stability Maintain the structural integrity of your building through regular inspections and repairs. Identify Safe Areas in Your Building Determine designated safe spots in your building, such as door frames or sturdy furniture. Educate Yourself on Building Evacuation Plans Familiarize yourself with the evacuation procedures and exit routes specific to your building.

During

Take Cover Under a Sturdy Object If possible, seek shelter under a sturdy object or in a strong doorway during a structural collapse. Protect Your Head and Neck Use your arms to cover and protect your head and neck from falling debris. Stay Calm and Await Assistance Remain calm, conserve energy, and wait for rescue personnel to reach you.

After

Call for Help if Trapped If you are trapped, use a phone or any available means to call for help and inform others of your situation. Follow Instructions from Rescue Personnel Cooperate with rescue personnel and follow their instructions for safe extraction or evacuation. Receive Medical Evaluation Seek immediate medical evaluation, even for seemingly minor injuries, as there may be internal trauma.

Before

Install Smoke Alarms Ensure working smoke alarms are installed on every level of your home. Create and Practice a Fire Escape Plan Establish an escape plan with clear routes and practice it regularly with your household members. Keep Flammable Materials Secure Store flammable materials safely and away from heat sources or open flames.

During

Evacuate Immediately If you discover a fire, evacuate the area immediately and close doors behind you to contain the fire. Crawl Low in Smoke-Filled Areas Crouch or crawl low to avoid inhaling smoke and toxic gases during evacuation. Use Stairs, Not Elevators Always use stairs for evacuation in case of a fire; avoid using elevators, which may malfunction.

After

Notify Emergency Services Call emergency services to report the fire, even if it has been extinguished. Do Not Re-enter the Building Avoid re-entering the building until it has been declared safe by fire officials. Contact Your Insurance Provider Notify your insurance provider of the fire and begin the claims process as soon as possible.
